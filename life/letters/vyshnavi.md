# Dear Vyshnavi
_Draft — April 2026_

I have been thinking a lot lately about how quickly life moves — and how, in the middle of all of it, you are the one constant that makes it feel meaningful.

You make me feel important every day. Not in a grand way, but in the way that matters most — you are just there. Whenever I need company, whenever I need someone beside me, you show up. Your presence is home to me. Even on my hardest days, just having you nearby is enough. We don't need to be doing anything — just being together is its own thing, and I have never felt this kind of quiet comfort with anyone else.

I genuinely love you. And I want you to know — I see you. Really see you.

You are the most loving person I have ever seen. The most loving, and the kindest. The way you make people feel heard — your friends, your family, my family — the way you prioritize people, the way you have empathy for everyone around you, human and animal alike. You see so much good in people. That is not a small thing. The world needs more people like you, and I want you to know that. I have never met anyone who loves as genuinely and freely as you do. Most people perform kindness — yours is just who you are. You give it without keeping score, and that is extraordinary.

You will be a great mother. A wonderful sister. A truly good daughter. I already know this — because I watch how you love, every single day.

There is one thing I want to say, gently, because I love you — I wish you would forgive your parents more. Deal with them with even more love. I know it is not always easy. But I say this because I see how much you are capable of giving, and I want that same grace to come back to you too. You deserve peace there.

And that brings me to something I have not said enough — you deserve to receive as much as you give. Your kindness is rare. The people around you are lucky, and I want you to truly believe that, not just hear it.

I have not always said these things out loud. I get caught up. I take the ordinary days — the companionship, the quiet evenings, the small things you do — for granted sometimes. I am aware of that. And I want to do better.

What I am committing to — with you — is a life that feels chosen. Building our family with intention. Slowing down enough to enjoy the ordinary days. Travelling, seeing the world together. Making sure we mark this life well, so that when we look back, it feels full.

You make me hopeful, Vyshnavi. Just by being who you are.

I love you.

Nitheesh
