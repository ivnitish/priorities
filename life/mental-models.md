# Mental Models — Charlie Munger
_6 core models with examples_

---

## 1. Inversion
Don't ask "how do I succeed?" Ask "what guarantees failure?" then avoid those things.

- **Investing:** instead of "which stock will go up?", ask "what kind of company destroys wealth?" — high debt, promoter fraud, commodity business. Avoid those.
- **Health:** instead of "how do I get fit?", ask "what habits guarantee I stay unfit?" — no sleep, no movement, poor food. Remove those first.
- **Marriage:** instead of "how do I be a good husband?", ask "what would a terrible husband do?" — ignore, take for granted, never express gratitude. Don't do those.

---

## 2. Circle of Competence
Know exactly what you understand deeply — and stay inside it. The edge matters more than the size.

- You understand Indian mid-cap businesses, consumer behavior, promoter quality. That's your circle.
- Crypto, global macro, options pricing — outside your circle. Not because they're bad, but because you can't reliably judge them.
- **Munger:** "It's not a sin to know little. It's a sin to not know what you don't know."

---

## 3. Second-Order Thinking
Everyone sees first-order effects. Ask what happens next, and next after that.

- **First order:** "This company's profit grew 30%." Everyone sees this.
- **Second order:** "At this growth rate they'll need to raise capital in 18 months, which dilutes shareholders."
- **Real life:** "I'll skip sleep to finish work tonight." Second order: tomorrow's focus is worse, you make a bad decision that costs more time than you saved.

---

## 4. Opportunity Cost
Every choice is a rejection of everything else. The real cost of anything is what you give up.

- Holding cash in savings at 4% when FD gives 7% — the cost isn't zero, it's 3% per year.
- Spending Sunday on a task that could be delegated — the cost is the family time you didn't take.
- **Munger's benchmark:** "Is this better than my best alternative?" If no, don't do it.

---

## 5. Margin of Safety
Always build in a buffer. Things go wrong. Assume they will.

- **Investing:** if a stock is worth 100, only buy at 60-70. The gap protects you from being wrong.
- **Life:** if the flight is at 9am, leave at 7am not 8:30am. The buffer absorbs what you can't predict.
- **Zanskar trip:** book tickets now, not 5 days before. The margin of safety against sold-out flights.

---

## 6. Lollapalooza Effect
When multiple forces align in the same direction, the outcome isn't additive — it's explosive.

- A great business + great management + right price + sector tailwind = not 4x better, potentially 20x.
- Opposite: bad habit + stress + poor sleep + bad diet hitting together — health collapses faster than any single factor explains.
- **When evaluating anything:** how many independent forces are aligned here?

---

## Daily Reminders
1. **Inversion** — "What would guarantee failure here? Avoid that."
2. **Opportunity cost** — "Is this the best use of this time/money?"
3. **Circle of competence** — "Do I actually understand this well enough to act?"
