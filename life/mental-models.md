# Mental Models — Charlie Munger
_10 core models. One rotates on the priorities page each week._

---

## 1. Inversion
Find what guarantees failure, then stop doing that.
*Stop adding tasks and start removing what's blocking the ones already there.*

## 2. Opportunity Cost
Every yes is a no to everything else.
*An evening on autopilot scrolling is an evening not spent with Vyshnavi or reading.*

## 3. Circle of Competence
Only act where you genuinely understand.
*You don't need a view on everything — fewer, deeper bets beat scattered opinions.*

## 4. Compounding
Small consistent actions over time beat large occasional ones.
*One Sovrenn video daily beats a weekend binge. One journal sentence nightly beats a monthly reflection.*

## 5. Second-Order Thinking
Think past the obvious first consequence.
*Skipping sleep to finish work tonight means worse decisions tomorrow — you lose more than you gained.*

## 6. Sunk Cost
Past investment is gone. Decide only on what happens from here.
*Holding a bad stock because you're down 30% costs you what it could have been elsewhere.*

## 7. Incentives
Understand what drives behavior — yours and theirs.
*You delay hard tasks not because you're lazy but because the brain avoids discomfort. Remove the friction, don't fight yourself.*

## 8. Margin of Safety
Build a buffer. Things will go wrong — assume they will.
*Book Zanskar tickets now, not a week before. The buffer absorbs what you can't predict.*

## 9. First Principles
Break the problem down to what's actually true, then rebuild.
*"I can't save 10-20k more" — is that actually true, or assumed? Open the bank app and find out.*

## 10. Social Proof
Don't follow the crowd without checking the reasoning.
*A stock everyone is buying is usually priced for perfection — check if the thesis holds, not just the momentum.*
